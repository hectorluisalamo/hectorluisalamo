## hey, I'm Hector 👋

Writer and editor by training.
AI systems architect by obsession.

I spent a decade in Latino media — as a managing editor at [Gozamos](https://gozamos.com), deputy editor at [Latino Rebels](https://latinorebels.com), and senior editor at [Futuro Media Group](https://futuromediagroup.org). That work shaped how I think: clarity matters, language matters, and systems always reflect values.

Then I taught myself to code through Harvard's CS50, earned certificates in data science and AI, and started building the kind of systems I used to work alongside in a newsroom. The throughline from writing to programming is simple: **words have always moved people... and now they also move machines**.

> 🥑 También desarrollo y colaboro profesionalmente [en español](README.es.md).

## what I'm building

- 🛡️ **[Palamo Studio](https://www.palamostudio.com)** — an AI governance and deployment consultancy I co-founded with my wife Rocio. We help mid-market companies adopt AI responsibly, with the measurement and accountability that boards and CFOs demand.
- 📰 **[The Crash Log](https://crashlog.ai)** — a daily bilingual newsletter about AI and tech gone off the rails. Produced by a governed agentic AI system I designed and edited by me. It's either the most honest thing in media right now or the most bleak. I think it's both.
- ⚙️ **Nico 2.0** — a production agentic system running 24/7 on a dedicated server. 13 scheduled tasks daily, its own memory system, voice calibration framework, and explicit governance guardrails. The AI behind The Crash Log.
- 📱 **ChicaFit** — a cross-platform fitness app for Spanish-speaking women, built with React Native and Expo.

## beliefs

- good software should be **useful, auditable, and built for the people it affects**
- small teams win because they're **nimble, curious, and not afraid of mistakes**
- AI is dangerous when deployed without governance, but powerful when it **translates human vision into reality with accountability**
- simplicity scales; over-engineering doesn't
- tech isn't for "some people" — it's for anyone with curiosity and drive

I reject gatekeeping, needless complexity, and the idea that technology has to feel cold.

I believe in the **beauty and boundless potential of humanity**.
